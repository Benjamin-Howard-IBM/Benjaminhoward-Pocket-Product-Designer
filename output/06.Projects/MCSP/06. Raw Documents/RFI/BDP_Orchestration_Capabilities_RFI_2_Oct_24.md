1) This Request for Information (RFI) is issued in accordance with the Federal Acquisition Regulation 
(FAR) clause 52.215-3 (Request for Information or Solicitation for Planning Purposes - Oct 1997) 
and is published to obtain information for use by the Air Force Lifecycle Management Center 
(AFLCMC), Cryptologic and Cyber Systems Division (CCSD), Joint Base San Antonio - Lackland, 
Texas, for market research and planning purposes only in support of the Development, Security and 
Operations (DevSecOps) of the Data Value Stream (DVS) for Unified Platform (UP). UP is 
responsible for developing and sustaining a cloud-based platform that enables cyber operations across 
the Department of Defense (DoD).

3) Our organization is currently leveraging Rapid Analytic Deployment and Management 
Framework (RADMF) for UP Big Data Platform (BDP) orchestration on AWS GovCloud. In an effort to 
identify potential RADMF alternatives, we are conducting market research to evaluate products and 
solutions that can provide the capabilities to meet the objectives and requirements listed in paragraph 4. 
The organizational objectives of this research are as follows:  
a) Identify and catalog available products and solutions in the market.  
 
b) Evaluate the features, functionality, and pricing of each alternative to determine its suitability for 
our needs.  
 
c) Assess the level of integration and compatibility of each alternative with our DevSecOps 
platform, while ensuring compliance with DoD security requirements. 
 
d) Estimate the level of government resources required to migrate to a new solution, while 
maintaining our current operational capabilities. 
4) The following are some objectives potential solution must help UP achieve: 
 
a. Cloud Provisioning – automated provisioning of a cloud-based multi-node platform 
capable of deploying clusters of over 15 petabytes in size.   
 
b. BDP automated deployment – Rapidly and automated provisioning of Big Data Platform 
Clusters through Continuous Integration/ Continuous Delivery (CI/CD) orchestrated

5) The following are some of the key characteristics that we are using to meet our organizational 
objectives, as outlined in paragraph 3. While this list is not exhaustive, it does provide a snapshot 
of some of the most important requirements for our environment: 
 
a. The solution shall provide orchestration/automation systems and tools which works for 
NIPRNet, SIPRNet and JWICS. 
i. The solution shall direct specific computers or virtual machines (VMs), 
networks, applications, and storage functions from end to end provisioning 
activities. 
ii. The solution shall provide auto-scaling functions to maintain a range of numbers 
of computers or VMs in services on the templates. 
iii. The solution shall have a web-based, remote management console and role-based 
control access. 
iv. The solution must be integrated with a web-based Enterprise Directory. 
b. The system shall support end-to-end provisioning capabilities 
i. The solution shall process infrastructure changes, requests to configuration 
management databases, and changes to subordinate orchestrators across multiple 
domains. 
ii. The solution shall support a specific range of numbers of computers or VMs, 
networks, applications, and data flows and storage. 
c. The system shall support automated provisioning of workflows 
i. The solution shall have a workflow design and customization capability. 
ii. The solution shall have the ability to override requests and/or stop a workflow as 
required by mission need. 
iii. The solution shall provide workflow features to make discovery processes more 
efficient. 
iv. The solution shall provide activity logs, including successful and failure 
activities. 
v. The solution shall provide acceptable performance, that it doesn’t impact or 
degrade existing system performance. 
vi. The solution shall work to provide partial system orchestration/automation 
functions without impacting the existing, active system’s services and 
performance. 
d. The system shall support resource provisioning, configuration, monitoring, scaling and 
removal. 
e. The system shall be deployed in a high availability architecture. 
 
6) Our organization will consider both commercial and open-source solutions.
